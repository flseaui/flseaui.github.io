    • Ironclad

      (2018 - 2019)

      Ironclad is a platform fighter I worked on for eight months with one other developer. Most of my time was spent working on rollback netcode and online lobbies (as we knew how much harder it could be to add later on) and a tool to let us visually design characters/moves, with the plan to eventually turn it into a player creation tool. Rollback requires complete determinism to function, and Unity isn't remotely deterministic, so solving that problem was very difficult and took far longer than we expected. We eventually finished an initial revision of the netcode but by that point it was clear that the scope of the project was far bigger than we could handle on our own so we put it on pause.

      • Had to ditch most Unity systems and used fixed-point math to keep determinism
      • Steamworks API integration for lobbies/matchmaking
      • Extensive story mode planned, introducing all eight of our playable characters
      • Made multiple iterations of our character design tool to keep it cross-platform
        • Multiple GUI frameworks were appealing, ended up settling on ImGui and MonoGame to simplify things after our WPF headache
        • Heavily considered making it a Unity extension
      • Struggled to find an artist we could afford, we planned to croudfund after finishing an MVP but never got to that point
      • At the time the genre was still niche but growing rapidly and we hoped to capitalize on that, we had many heavily desired features planned that didn't yet exist in competitors (but now do)

      • Won Oregon high school game competition

  • ABOUT

    I've been a creator for as long as I can remember, when I was young it was drawings, stories, stop-motion animations, but for the past 10 or so years it has been games. I've always been fascinated by games and have spent much of my life figuring out how they work and creating my own. I initially started by making small Minecraft mods and loved getting to express myself through a medium I cared so much about, as I spent more time focused on making games I realized how much I loved programming too, the problem solving, logic, collaboration, there's nothing quite like it. I love the satisfaction of architecting the perfect solution to a software problem and am always looking for a new challenge. I have experience with many languages and frameworks and can quickly pick up new ones. I'm motivated and driven by a desire to bring people joy and have fun doing it.

    As motivated as I've been I felt unsupported by my environment for the longest time, I was unengaged with and uninterested in school (and was going through a very stressful time of gender discovery), and decided to stop attending in 2018 to fully persue indie game development, I quickly got a GED then started Broken Record Studios with a friend of mine. Though our two games Ironclad and Ocular never saw the light of day I learned and improved so much and feel fully prepared for whatever is next.

    When not programming you can probably find me tinkering with various other creative projects, playing games, rock climbing, or spending time with friends.
